Something Extra

It has become a habit of mine when travelling to sign up for a cookery course, I find this often brings back holiday memories better than photos. I spent Christmas 2014 travelling around Sri Lanka and between torrential rain and mudslides on Christmas Day in the tea plantations of Nuwara Eliya, and whale watching off Mirissa I took ??? cooking class in Unawatuna and was introduced to this recipe for sunshine in a bowl. Traditionally in Sri Lanka a variety of curries are eaten in small portions, in the photo below the pineapple curry is at the 6 o'clock position.

Sri Lankan pineapple curry

  • 1 pineapple in small pieces
  • 1 med red onion chopped
  • 4 garlic cloves sliced
  • 20 curry leaves torn (dry leaves will work but won’t look as vibrant)
  • 1 inch of cinnamon stick or small pinch of powder
  • 50ml coconut milk (make from powder as it’s a small amount)

    • Spices (measures in teaspoons)

  • 1 curry powder
  • ½ salt
  • ½ black pepper
  • 1 ½ red chili flakes (or more)
  • ¼ garam masala
  • ¼ turmeric

    • Fry the onion and garlic until soft then add the pineapple, cinnamon and curry leaves and cook for a further 3-4 minutes. Then add all your remaining spices and add 50ml of coconut cream. Simmer for 10 minutes (ish) before serving add a teaspoon of sugar or a squirt of agave syrup if aspiring middle class like me.

    Enjoy
